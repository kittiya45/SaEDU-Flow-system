# Product

## Register

product

## Users

นิสิต กนค. (student committee members), อาจารย์ที่ปรึกษา (faculty advisors), and เจ้าหน้าที่ (administrative staff) at a Thai university. All three groups use this manual page. Students are the primary audience — many are new to formal document workflows. Teachers and staff are secondary. Users are Thai-speaking and access the system on desktop browsers, typically during daytime working hours.

## Product Purpose

SAEDU Flow is an electronic document management system (ระบบเสนอเอกสารอิเล็กทรอนิกส์) for the student committee (กนค.). It handles the full lifecycle of institutional documents: drafting, review, signing, numbering, and archival. The manual.html is the reference guide that helps all three user groups understand how to use the system confidently. Success means a new student committee member can onboard without needing to ask anyone.

## Brand Personality

สะอาด (clean), เชื่อถือได้ (trustworthy), เป็นมิตร (approachable). Professional enough for an academic institution but warm enough for student users. Thai-first, not a Western SaaS reskin.

## Anti-references

- Old Thai government systems (dense tables, Times New Roman, วิธีใช้งานหน้าจอ aesthetic)
- Overly foreign SaaS (English-defaulting, neon accents, startup-speak copy)
- Heavy and intimidating (complex navigation, modal-heavy, jargon-filled)
- Flashy / presentation-mode (slide-like sections, excessive animation, gradient-text)

## Design Principles

1. **Thai-first**: Typography, spacing, and tone choices that feel native to Thai readers, not translated.
2. **Confidence without weight**: Authoritative enough for institutional use, light enough that a first-year student doesn't feel intimidated.
3. **Clarity over cleverness**: Every UI element earns its place. Decoration that doesn't help the user find what they need should be removed.
4. **Roles are real**: The three-role structure is load-bearing. The interface should make it obvious which instructions apply to you, without making other roles feel hidden.
5. **Print-ready as a first-class concern**: This is a reference manual. Users will print it or PDF it. The design must hold up without color.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Thai text requires larger line-height (1.7+) and slightly more generous font sizes than Latin text for comfortable reading. Avoid relying solely on color to convey status — always pair with icon or text label.
